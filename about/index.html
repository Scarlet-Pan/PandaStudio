Why we built this

Two real incidents showed the gap between “having a policy” and “being able to use it when it matters.”

A sprained ankle

The family held two accident policies with different roles. At the clinic it was unclear which hospital tier applied — one case was settled by goodwill, which is not a plan for next time.

A spouse’s illness in pregnancy

When the person who manages the policies is the patient, nobody else can read exclusions and care paths from dense PDFs under stress.

为什么做

两次真实经历，让我们看见「有保单」和「关键时刻用得上」之间的鸿沟。

契机一 · 脚崴就医

家里配了两份意外险，职责分明；真崴脚时分工早已记不清，社区医院是否符合「二级及以上」也难即时判断——那一次靠通融，但不能指望下次。

契机二 · 配偶孕期上感

管保单的人倒下时，特需路径与妊娠免责难判断；PDF 在手机里，紧急时刻翻不动。
